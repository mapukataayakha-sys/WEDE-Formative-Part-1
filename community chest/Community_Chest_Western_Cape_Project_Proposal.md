# PROJECT PROPOSAL: COMMUNITY CHEST OF THE WESTERN CAPE - WEBSITE REDESIGN

## ORGANISATION OVERVIEW
**Organization Name:** Community Chest of the Western Cape  
**Established:** 1928  
**Location:** 82 Bree Street, Cape Town City Centre, Cape Town 8000, South Africa  
**History:** Community Chest was founded in 1928 as the Cape Town Welfare Board, making it one of the oldest civil society organizations in South Africa. We provide training, conduct community conversations, and make grants and in-kind donations to organizations working across the Western Cape province.

**Mission:** To improve the lives of all South Africans by mobilising the caring power of communities, business and government to advance the common good.

**Vision:** A world where all individuals and families achieve their human potential through access to world-class education, social and economic justice, income progress, and access to a healthy lifestyle.

**Target Audience:** NGO leaders seeking grants and capacity building, donors wanting to support community development, corporate partners, community development practitioners, and organizations in marginalized communities across South Africa working in health, education, community development, and income generation sectors.

## WEBSITE GOALS & OBJECTIVES
**Primary Goals:**
- Make it easier for NGO leaders to find and apply for grants—we currently get calls every week from organizations that didn't know they were eligible for certain funding streams
- Reduce the time our admin team spends answering basic questions about eligibility and deadlines
- Show potential donors exactly where their money goes—right now our annual report sits as a PDF that almost no one reads
- Give training participants a single place to access course materials, calendars, and certificates
- Capture visitor information so we can build a mailing list for grant deadlines and training announcements

**Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):**
- Monthly website traffic: 5,000+ visits (we don't currently track this, so this is an educated guess based on similar nonprofit sites)
- Grant application submissions: 35+ per month (we average 25 now, hoping to bump this with clearer deadlines)
- Donor inquiries: 15+ monthly (currently most donor inquiries come through word-of-mouth)
- Training course registrations: 40+ participants per cohort (our last training cohort was 32 people)
- Newsletter subscribers: 1,200+ within 6 months (we have about 600 now through Mailchimp)
- Page load time: <3 seconds on mobile (critical for users in township areas with slower connections)

## CURRENT WEBSITE ANALYSIS
Our current site at comchest.org.za was built in 2018 and has served us reasonably well, but it's showing its age:

- The grant application section is buried—we've had applicants miss deadlines because they couldn't find the information easily
- Most of our traffic comes from mobile, but the current site wasn't designed mobile-first
- We don't have a simple way for past grant recipients to share updates or success stories
- The "Apply for Funding" button is hidden in the navigation and gets confused with general contact info
- Our impact statistics (R14.2 million granted last year, 147 organizations funded) are scattered across different pages and hard to find
- Several board members have mentioned the site looks "dated" compared to newer NGO sites they've seen

## PROPOSED WEBSITE FEATURES
- **Homepage:** Clear call-to-action for grant applications, current grant deadlines, impact statistics at a glance, rotating spotlight on 2-3 funded organizations
- **About Us:** Our 96-year history, current board and staff, how we make funding decisions, our relationship with the National Community Chest
- **Grant Programs:** Separate pages for each funding stream (health, education, community development, income generation), with clear eligibility criteria, typical award amounts, and common reasons applications are declined
- **Training Hub:** Upcoming courses, downloadable materials for past participants, how to register, what to expect from each training program
- **Impact Stories:** Case studies from funded organizations, with photos and quotes from the people actually running these projects—not just numbers
- **Donor Portal:** How to donate, what organizations your funding supports, our annual report in a readable format, corporate partnership options
- **Resource Library:** Application templates, previous successful applications (anonymized), best practice guides, report templates for current grantees
- **News & Events:** Upcoming community conversations, grant deadlines, links to our social media
- **Contact & Support:** Direct contact for each department, FAQ based on questions we actually receive, application checklist

## DESIGN & USER EXPERIENCE
**Aesthetic Direction:** We want the site to feel like a credible, established organization—not flashy, but professional and warm. We're not trying to look like a tech startup. Many of our users are older NGO leaders who are comfortable with traditional layouts, so we want something intuitive rather than trendy.

**Design Elements:**
- **Color Scheme:** Navy (#1B3A8A), green (#2D7A4C), gold (#D4A844)—something that feels official but welcoming. We'd like to keep our existing colors rather than do a complete rebrand
- **Typography:** Clean, readable. Nothing fancy. We want visitors to feel the site respects their time
- **Layout:** Clear hierarchy with important actions (Apply for Funding, Donate, Contact) immediately visible. We're tired of users telling us they couldn't find how to apply
- **User Experience:** Working grant search is critical—users should be able to filter by sector, amount, and deadline. Mobile is priority since so many of our users access from their phones. Accessibility matters—several of our partner organizations work with visually impaired communities

**Low-Fidelity Wireframes:** We have sketches for the homepage, grant directory with filters, and training calendar. Happy to share these in our next meeting.

## TECHNICAL REQUIREMENTS
- **Languages:** HTML5, CSS3, JavaScript—nothing too complex, we want to be able to maintain this ourselves going forward
- **Hosting:** We're currently on shared hosting and it slows down. Looking at something more reliable, probably AWS or a similar provider
- **Domain:** comchest.org.za (must keep this)
- **Mobile Responsive:** Essential. Our Google Analytics shows 68% mobile traffic
- **Security:** Basic SSL, protection against spam submissions on contact forms
- **Integrations:** Mailchimp for newsletters (we already use this), potential to add a donation gateway later if the board approves
